# Idea Refine

## Overview
Transform vague concepts into actionable specifications through structured questioning and iterative refinement.

## Process

### 1. Surface the Core
Ask: "What problem are we solving?" and "Who is the user?"

### 2. Identify Constraints
- Technical constraints (stack, hosting, budget)
- Business constraints (timeline, compliance, scale)
- User constraints (devices, languages, accessibility)

### 3. Explore Options
For each approach, list: pros, cons, effort estimate, risks.

### 4. Define Success Criteria
Replace "make it better" with concrete metrics:
- "Dashboard loads in < 2s"
- "User can complete donation in 3 clicks"

### 5. Document Decisions
Capture what was decided and why, including rejected alternatives.

## Questions to Ask
- What does "done" look like?
- What's the minimum viable version?
- Who will use this and how?
- What could go wrong?
- How will we measure success?

## Verification
- [ ] Core problem is clearly stated
- [ ] Success criteria are measurable
- [ ] Constraints are documented
- [ ] Rejected alternatives are noted
